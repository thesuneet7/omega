# GTM — first region, who to approach, and how (aligned with ICP)

This document turns the people described in **[ICP.md](ICP.md)** into a practical **go-to-market** view: **where to start geographically**, **who can actually buy** (and why “sell to PM” is the wrong mental model early), and **which distribution channels** fit a local-first desktop product sold by a small team.

It does **not** replace ICP.md — it **operationalizes** the same wedge: *mid-level knowledge workers doing intensive session-based research who need a structured artifact at the end*.

---

## 1. Anchor: what the ICP already decided

From [ICP.md](ICP.md), the **primary** fit is:

- **Independent consultants, strategy/market analysts, and related knowledge workers** — strongest wedge (client confidentiality, multi-tab research, deliverable pressure).
- **PMs and UX researchers** — **co-equal on workflow fit**, but **not** the easiest *first* procurement motion (see §3).

Core job (same document):

> Turn a messy multi-hour research session into a **structured, shareable artifact** — automatically, **local-first** where that matters.

Everything below assumes you **prove the loop** with people who feel this pain weekly and can **pay with a card** without a six-month enterprise cycle.

## 2. Which country / region to target first

### Recommended first focus: **United States**

| Reason | Why it matters for Omega |
| ------ | ------------------------ |
| **Depth of addressable roles** | Huge pool of independent consultants, boutique firms, in-house strategy/research, and tech PMs who match the ICP. |
| **Language** | Your product UX and marketing are likely English-first; US is the largest **English** concentration of the ICP. |
| **Payment behavior** | Corporate cards, expensed SaaS, and willingness to pay for **individual** productivity tools are well established. |
| **Time zones (if you sell live)** | Easier to do founder calls, user interviews, and support in one dominant zone while you learn. |
| **Regulatory narrative** | “Local-first / client data stays on device” resonates with US consulting and tech buyers who already worry about leakage. |

### Second wave (same playbook, translate less)

When the US loop works (repeatable onboarding, support, pricing), expand in order of **least friction**:

1. **United Kingdom, Canada, Australia** — English, similar B2B SaaS norms, strong consulting and tech sectors.
2. **EU (English-speaking GTM)** — Ireland, Netherlands, Nordics (often English workflows); plan for **VAT** and privacy copy (GDPR) before heavy spend; payments handled by MoR or Stripe Tax (see [PAYMENTS_AND_PRICING_SETUP.md](PAYMENTS_AND_PRICING_SETUP.md)).

### What to defer (unless *you* are there)

- **India / Southeast Asia / LATAM** — large talent pools, but **price sensitivity**, expensing norms, and mix of Windows vs Mac vary; better as **wave 3** with localized pricing and support capacity — not because the ICP doesn’t exist, but because **CAC + pricing** need a separate thesis.

### One line to use internally

**Start US → English-speaking mature SaaS markets → then broaden.**

## 3. “You can’t sell directly to a PM” — what that actually means

Job title **“Product Manager”** is not a **budget owner** or a **procurement process** in most companies. No one answers a cold email “Dear PM, buy our screen capture tool” with a PO.

What is true instead:

| Reality | Implication |
| ------- | ----------- |
| **PM is a persona**, not a **buying committee**. | Early revenue comes from **people who can say yes with a credit card** in one step. |
| **Pain is individual**; **budget is often team or personal**. | Target **ICs and leads** who already buy Notion, Linear, Superhuman, etc. |
| **Enterprise PM** needs **security review** — too heavy for v1. | Defer “sell to Figma’s PM org” until you have **trust artifacts** (SOC2 path, DPAs, etc.). |

So: **you are not “selling to PM as a department.”** You are **selling to humans who match the PM/analyst/consultant workflow** where they already hang out and buy tools.

**Who to target *first* (ordered):**

1. **Independent / boutique consultants** — buyer = themselves; fastest feedback; ICP.md names this as the **wedge**.
2. **Analysts / researchers** (corporate strategy, market research, due diligence boutiques) — often **expensed** under “software” with light approval.
3. **Senior ICs / leads** in product and design (including “PM” title) who **personally** adopt and later **pull** team interest — not **top-down** enterprise sales on day one.

**PMs** become a **channel** through **content and community** (they discover you), not through **org-wide deals** at first.

## 4. How to start approaching customers (early-stage motions)

These pair with **[JUST_RELEASE_MINIMAL_COST.md](JUST_RELEASE_MINIMAL_COST.md)** — no enterprise stack required.

| Motion | What you do | Fits ICP because |
| ------ | ----------- | ---------------- |
| **Founder-led user interviews** | 20–30 min calls with people who match the ICP; **no pitch deck** — validate session pain and confidentiality concerns. | Consultants and analysts **talk**; you learn vocabulary for copy and trust. |
| **Dogfood + case study** | One credible story: “Here’s a 3-hour research session → summary I used in a client memo.” | Proof beats features for **local-first** skepticism. |
| **Community presence** | Thoughtful posts where **research pain** is discussed (LinkedIn, X, niche Slacks) — not spam. | ICP lives in **professional** feeds, not App Store search alone. |
| **Warm intros** | Ask your network: “Who does competitive intel or deck research for hours?” | Boutiques and freelancers cluster by referral. |
| **Lightweight outbound** | Short, specific notes to **boutique consulting firms** or **solo practices** (not Fortune 500). | Smaller orgs = faster “try it Tuesday.” |

**Avoid early:** RFPs, procurement portals, “pilot with IT” — that’s **later** B2B.

## 5. Distribution channels (realistic for a desktop, local-first tool)

| Channel | Role | Notes |
| ------- | ---- | ----- |
| **Website + GitHub Releases** (or signed download) | **Canonical** place to download and read trust story. | Align with [BUILD_AND_DISTRIBUTION.md](BUILD_AND_DISTRIBUTION.md). |
| **LinkedIn** (organic + selective DM) | Reach **consultants, analysts, PMs** where they work identities live. | Lead with **problem**, not product jargon; no mass automation spam. |
| **X / Twitter** | **Build in public**, research workflow tips, privacy-local narrative. | Good for indie consultant + tech IC overlap. |
| **Newsletter / podcast adjacency** | Sponsorships or **guest** spots on **consulting, product, research** adjacent shows — when you have a clear one-liner from ICP. | Expensive sponsors later; **free** if you’re a guest with a story. |
| **Communities** | Slack/Discord groups for **strategy, product, UX research** (often paid communities = higher intent). | Read rules; add value before links. |
| **Product Hunt** | One **launch moment**; spikes traffic, not sustained GTM alone. | Pair with email capture and follow-up. |
| **SEO (long-term)** | “Competitive research session”, “client confidential research notes”, “local-first research tool” — **months** to compound. | Start early with **one** strong landing page. |
| **Partnerships (later)** | Course creators, template sellers, **Notion/obsidian-adjacent** educators who teach research workflows. | Revenue share or affiliate; only after product is stable. |

**Not primary channels for v1:** Mac App Store as *only* channel (review, sandbox friction for capture); **paid ads** before you know CAC and message-market fit.

## 6. Synthesis: first 90 days (example)

| Week band | Geography | Who | Channel |
| --------- | --------- | --- | ------- |
| **1–4** | **US** (plus your own timezone for calls) | Independent consultants + 1–2 analyst profiles | Founder interviews, LinkedIn warm intros |
| **5–8** | Same | Add **IC PM/UXR** who self-identify with “long research blocks” | Communities + one **case study** |
| **9–12** | Add **UK/CA** if inbound appears | Same ICP | Website clarity, waitlist, **Product Hunt** only if stable |

Success is not “signed Fortune 500.” It is **repeatable weekly use** and **paid conversions** from people matching [ICP.md](ICP.md).
